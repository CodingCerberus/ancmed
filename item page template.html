Terracotta ship model

Cat. No. C 15 Date LC I/II Findspot Enkomi, unknown context (tomb ?) Dimensions L: 18 cm;
beam amidships: 6 cm;
height amidships: 4 cm;
height from gunwale to top of stem: 5.5 cm Medium Terracotta model. Reddish-brown clay. Faint traces of dark brown paint on one side, in the form of two parallel bands that converge on one end. Parts of a post broken and reconstructed, some damage to gunwale. Accession Number Musées Royaux d’Art et d’Histoire, Brussels A 1240 References Basch 1987: 73-74, no. 146; Gray 1974: G.20, 61; Göttlicher 1977: 35, no. 150, pl. 9; Knapp 2019: 142, no. 4; Merrillees 1968: 188, pl. 37:1; Wachsmann 1998: 50-51, fig. 3.15; Westerberg 1983: 15, 83, fig. 9

Crescent shaped hull with high, symmetrical vertical posts ending in a flat, undecorated top. The external edge of the posts is slightly concave, which is typical for Syro-Canaanite ships. There are no interior fittings. Faint traces of two bands of paint on one side of the hull that were not noted in previous publications.

Although uncertain, it is though that the model is from Enkomi. The relatively good level of preservation suggests that it probably came from a tomb.

The ship bears resemblance to later boat models from Achziv, but Enkomi’s abandonment at the end of the Late Bronze Age argues against an early Iron Age date. The model depicts a mnš ship, a beamy merchant vessel of Levantine origin that is attested for over a millennium, from the 18th to the 7th century B.C. in the Levant itself, but also on Cyprus and in Egypt.
